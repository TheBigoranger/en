Math facts

Norm

Definition: For vector $x\in\mathbb{R}^n$, $p\in(0,\infty]$

When $p\in[1,\infty]$, $|x|_p$ is a norm.

Theorem: $\left|x\right|_p\leq |x|_q,\forall p\geq q>0$.

Proof: Consider the auxiliary vectors $y=\frac{|x|}{\left|x\right|_q}$. Note that each entry of $y$ is less than 1:
\begin{gather*}
y_k=\frac{|x_k|}{\left|x\right|_q}\leq 1\
\Rightarrow y_k^p\geq y_k^q,\ \forall k=1,\cdots,n.
\end{gather*}

Control Theory

Stability

Definition: A square matrix $A\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ is Hurwitz-stable iff.
\begin{gather*}
Re[\lambda_i(A)]<0,\ \forall i=1,\cdots,n
\end{gather*}

Theorem(Lyapunov Stability): $A\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ is Hurwitz-stable iff. there exists $P\succ 0$ such that
\begin{gather}
PA+A^TP\prec 0.
\end{gather}
